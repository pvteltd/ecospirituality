Deen al-Fitrah

Deen al-Fitrah, or ecospirituality, goes beyond mere comprehension of the bond among nature, spirituality, and mankind. It acknowledges the divine pact of kingship, where God is the Trustor, and ecospiritualists are the Trustees, with all creatures being the beneficiaries. This pledge is mirrored in the Law of Trust, which directs our association with the surroundings, combined with the Universal Laws governing the cosmos. Here, on this website, we will delve into the correlation between Ecospirituality, the Law of Trust, Universal Laws, and the teachings of the Quran.

JOIN NATURE'S GOD MOVEMENT

Divine Kingship

In the sacred teachings of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, the concept of divine kingship is deeply rooted. It is a belief that God is the ultimate authority and that humans are responsible for governing the Earth according to His divine will. This belief is reflected in the holy books of the Torah, Injeel, and Quran, where God has given humanity a covenant. This covenant outlines our responsibility as Trustees of the Earth, tasked with the noble duty of caring for the environment and all its inhabitants. It is through this covenant that the right of divine kingship is established, and as we are reminded, "We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors, we borrow it from our children."

Schools of Jurisprudence

The foundation of Trust Law lies in the Natural Law School of Jurisprudence. Our responsibility to care for the environment and uphold natural rights is not a privilege bestowed upon us by human authority, but rather an inherent part of our relationship with the environment and Nature's God. Those who oppose the Natural Law School of Jurisprudence, including the Analytical, Historical or Sociological, and Law & Economic schools, may argue that human authority is the only legitimate source of law and that Moral Absolutism has no place in the law. But naturalists believe that the law is based on timeless principles discovered through observation, not created by humans. On the other hand, humanists believe that the law is a product of human society and its values.

Sacred Economics

The gift economy is a principle woven into the very fabric of the natural world. The sun, the air, the trees, and the animals all freely give of themselves to sustain life, without seeking anything in return. This selfless giving is an embodiment of the Universal Laws of Divine Oneness, Law of Vibration, Law of Attraction, Law of Action, Law of Correspondence, Law of Attraction, Law of Cause and Effect, Law of Perpetual Transmutation of Energy, and many more. Embracing the gift economy is essential to our attainment of these laws, leading us toward harmonious existence for all beings and enlightenment.

Where do rights come from?

In the realm of ecospirituality, the pursuit of truth holds a significant place, especially in the context of the Natural Law that shapes our connection with the environment. The Natural Law, which encompasses the laws of nature governing the universe, the natural person, and the natural society, is rooted in three fundamental themes. These themes comprise the Law of Nature, Natural Science, and Metaphysics, which explore the essence of the natural world; the Natural Person, Formal Science, and Epistemology, which focus on the nature of human beings; and the Natural Society, Social Science, and Axiology, which delve into the dynamics of human societies.

TAKE THE TOUR

Law of Nature

The Law of Nature is a powerful force that governs the universe, from the smallest subatomic particles to the largest celestial bodies. Its objective and immutable nature is a testament to the beauty and intricacy of the natural world. Through the study of Natural Science, we seek to unlock the mysteries of this law, gaining insight into the workings of the universe and our place within it. And as we delve deeper into the realm of Metaphysics, we contemplate the very essence of reality itself, pondering the interplay between the physical and spiritual dimensions of existence. Together, these three themes paint a picture of a universe that is both awe-inspiring and intimately intertwined with our own being.

Natural Person

In ecospirituality, the concept of the Natural Person is essential to understanding our relationship with the environment and the Natural Law. This aspect explores human nature and how it relates to the laws of nature. Formal Science, such as mathematics and logic, plays a crucial role in understanding the properties and relationships of abstract objects and concepts that are inherent in the Natural Law. Epistemology is another important aspect of ecospirituality as it delves into the nature of knowledge and how humans can acquire it. It enables us to distinguish illusion from reality and understand how we perceive the world around us. Through the study of the Natural Person, Formal Science, and Epistemology, we can gain a deeper understanding of the Natural Law and our place within it.

Natural Society

In the realm of ecospirituality, the Natural Society plays a crucial role in understanding our relationship with the environment and with each other. Social Science offers us the tools to examine and understand human society, including its institutions, relationships, and behaviors. Meanwhile, Axiology studies our values and ethical principles, helping us to recognize the importance of natural rights, which are rooted in the natural science and formal science. By acknowledging these natural rights, we can build a foundation for a just and sustainable society that honors the interconnectedness of all beings.

Right Context

The profound and compelling statement, "We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors, we borrow it from our children," encapsulates the principle of intergenerational equity, responsibility, and stewardship. It acknowledges that the Earth is not ours to own or exploit through the Rule by Law, but rather a sacred trust that we are honored to safeguard for future generations. Understanding the Natural Law is pivotal to our comprehension of truth and to guide our relationship with the environment. The Natural Law provides a framework to grasp the interconnectedness between nature, spirituality, and humanity. It also emphasizes the importance of the covenant of Divine Kingship and the Law of Trust, which obliges us to care for the Earth and its inhabitants. By recognizing the Natural Law, we can attain a profound awareness of our place in the universe and our role as stewards of God's creation.
