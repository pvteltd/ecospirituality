Ecospirituality

Ecospirituality is about more than just understanding the connection between nature, spirituality, and humanity. It also recognizes the special covenant of Divine Kingship, where God is the Trustor, and ecospiritualists are the Trustees, with all living things as beneficiaries. This covenant is reflected in the Law of Trust, which guides our relationship with the environment, along with the Universal Laws that govern the universe. On this website, we will explore the link between ecospirituality, the Law of Trust, the Universal Laws, and the teachings of the Quran.

JOIN NATURE'S GOD MOVEMENT

Divine Kingship

The concept of divine kingship is rooted in the belief that God is the ultimate authority and that human beings are responsible for governing the Earth in accordance with God's will. This belief is shared by many religious traditions, including Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. According to these traditions, God gave humanity a covenant through the holy books of the Torah, Injeel, and Quran. This covenant outlines our responsibility as Trustees of the Earth, and our duty to care for the environment and all its inhabitants. It is through this covenant that the right of divine kingship is established; "We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors, we borrow it from our children."

Schools of Jurisprudence

The concept of Trust Law is rooted in the Natural Law School of Jurisprudence. This responsibility is not something that is granted to us by human authority, but rather is an inherent part of our relationship with the environment and Nature's God. Opponents of the Natural Law School of Jurisprudence, such as the Analytical School of Jurisprudence, the Historical or Sociological Jurisprudence, and the Law & Economic School of Jurisprudence, may argue that human authority is the only legitimate source of law and that Moral Absolutism and its ethical principles have no place in the Law. Naturalists believe that the law is based on natural principles and is discovered, not created. On the other hand, humanists believe that law is a product of human society and its values.

The Principles of Truth

The formulation of truth is an essential component of the concept of ecospirituality, particularly when considering the role of the Natural Law in guiding humanity's relationship with the environment. The Natural Law can be defined as the law of nature that governs the universe, the natural person, and the natural society. It is based on three major themes, including the Law of Nature, Natural Science, Metaphysics, the Natural Person, Formal Science, Epistemology, and the Natural Society, Social Science, Axiology.

TAKE THE TOUR

Law of Nature

The Law of Nature pertains to the natural laws that govern the universe, including the laws of physics, chemistry, and biology. These laws are objective and immutable, and they govern all aspects of the natural world. Natural Science, on the other hand, is the study of these natural laws, which aims to understand the workings of the universe and how it relates to human existence. Metaphysics is the branch of philosophy that seeks to understand the fundamental nature of reality, including the nature of existence, the universe, and the relationship between the physical and spiritual realms.

Natural Person

The Natural Person pertains to human nature and how it relates to the Natural Law. Formal Science, such as mathematics and logic, is used to study the properties and relationships of abstract objects and concepts. Epistemology is the study of knowledge, including the nature of knowledge and how it is acquired. It also considers how humans perceive reality and how they can understand the world around them.

Natural Society

The Natural Society pertains to the relationship between humans and their social environment, including their communities and cultures. Social Science studies human society, including its institutions, relationships, and behaviors. Axiology is the study of values and value systems, including ethical and moral principles.

Right Context

The major premise "We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors, we borrow it from our children" is a powerful statement that highlights the idea of intergenerational equity, responsibility, and stewardship. It implies that the Earth is not something that we can claim ownership of through the Rule by Law, but rather something that we are entrusted with for a limited time. In this context, understanding the Natural Law is crucial for formulating truth and guiding humanity's relationship with the environment. The Natural Law provides a framework for understanding the interconnectedness between nature, spirituality, and humanity. It also highlights the importance of the covenant for Divine Kingship and the Law of Trust, which acknowledges humanity's responsibility to care for the Earth and all its inhabitants. By understanding the Natural Law, humanity can achieve a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and their role as stewards of God's creation.
